Taiwanese Spicy Beef Noodle Soup


Note: This recipe is a copy of the AllRecipes "Taiwanese Spicy Beef Noodle Soup" by jhuang0125.

Description

This is a simple, low effort recipe made in a slow cooker. The recipe takes 8 hours and 40 minutes in total, with 25 minutes of prep time and 8 hours and 15 minutes of cook time. All that's needed is a little time preparing the ingredients. Then let the slow cooker do all the work for you during the day. It makes 8 servings, with each serving packing 482 calories, 42g carbs, and 27g protein, enough to feed the whole family and more!

These Taiwanese spicy beef noodles are sure to not only warm you up on a cold winter day, but will also bring out authentic flavors of one of the most iconic dishes from Taiwan, leaving you craving for more. Enjoy this small token of Taiwanese culture!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil (or more as needed)
  • 8 cups water (or more as needed)
  • One 14 oz can beef broth
  • 1 bunch green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup rice wine
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 10 cloves garlic, peeled (or more to taste)
  • 3 small chile peppers, halved and seeded (or more to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons chile paste (or to taste)
  • One 1 1/2 inch piece fresh ginger, peeled and cut into 5 pieces
  • 3 star anise pods (or more to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • 4 small heads baby bok choy
  • One 10 oz package udon noodles
  • (Optional) 1 tablespoon chopped pickled mustard greens (or to taste)

Steps

  1. Place beef in a stockpot and cover with water; bring to a boil. Remove from the heat and drain.
  2. Pour vegetable oil into a slow cooker; add beef, 8 cups water, broth, green onions, soy sauce, rice wine, brown sugar, garlic, chile peppers, chile paste, ginger, star anise, and five-spice powder. Cover and cook on Low for 8 to 9 hours.
  3. Transfer beef to a bowl with a slotted spoon. Strain broth into a bowl; discard solids. Return broth to the slow cooker; keep on Warm until needed.
  4. Bring a pot of water to a boil; add bok choy and cook for 30 seconds. Remove with a slotted spoon and run under cold water. Drain, then roughly chop.
  5. Add udon noodles to boiling water; cook until tender yet firm to the bite, about 4 minutes. Drain.
  6. Divide noodles among serving bowls and top with beef, bok choy, and hot broth. Garnish with mustard greens.
